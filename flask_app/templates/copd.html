COPD

Understanding Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease: Symptoms, causes, and management of this progressive lung condition.

Comparison of Healthy Lungs vs. COPD-Affected Lungs

üß¨ Introduction

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a chronic inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs. It's typically caused by long-term exposure to irritating gases or particulate matter, most often from cigarette smoke. COPD includes two main conditions: emphysema and chronic bronchitis.

ü´Å How It Affects the Lungs

COPD damages the airways and alveoli in the lungs, causing them to lose their elasticity and over-expand, which leaves air trapped in the lungs when you exhale. In emphysema, the walls between many of the air sacs are destroyed. In chronic bronchitis, the lining of the airways is constantly irritated and inflamed, leading to thickening and increased mucus production.

Did you know? COPD is the third leading cause of death worldwide, affecting an estimated 384 million people.

üß™ Causes & Risk Factors

Primary causes and contributing factors:

Smoking

Accounts for about 85-90% of COPD cases

Environmental Factors

Long-term exposure to air pollution, chemical fumes, dust

Genetic Factors

Alpha-1-antitrypsin deficiency (rare genetic disorder)

Biomass Fuel Exposure

Common in developing countries from cooking/heating fires

üõ°Ô∏è Management & Treatment

Medications

  • Bronchodilators (short and long-acting)
  • Inhaled corticosteroids
  • Combination inhalers
  • Phosphodiesterase-4 inhibitors

Therapies

  • Pulmonary rehabilitation
  • Oxygen therapy
  • Non-invasive ventilation
  • Lung volume reduction surgery

Lifestyle Changes

  • Smoking cessation (most critical)
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy nutrition
  • Avoiding air pollutants

Prevention

  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Workplace safety measures
  • Annual flu vaccine
  • Pneumococcal vaccines

üè• When to Seek Emergency Care

Seek immediate medical attention for:

  • Severe shortness of breath
  • Blue or gray lips/fingernails
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Confusion or drowsiness
  • Symptoms not relieved by medications
