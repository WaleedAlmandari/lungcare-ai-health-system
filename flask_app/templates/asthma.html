Asthma

Understanding asthma: symptoms, triggers, management, and treatment of this chronic respiratory condition.

Visual Comparison of Normal vs. Asthmatic Airways

üß¨ Introduction

Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways that causes recurring episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing. During an asthma attack, the airways become inflamed and narrowed, making breathing difficult. Asthma affects people of all ages but often starts in childhood.

ü´Å How It Affects the Airways

Asthma causes three main changes in the airways: inflammation (swelling), bronchoconstriction (muscle tightening), and increased mucus production. These changes narrow the airways, making it harder for air to flow in and out of the lungs. Over time, chronic inflammation can lead to permanent airway remodeling.

Did you know? Asthma affects about 1 in 13 people in the United States, including over 5 million children.

üîµ Symptoms

Wheezing

High-pitched whistling sound when breathing out

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing or feeling breathless

Chest Tightness

Feeling like something is squeezing your chest

Coughing

Often worse at night or early morning

Fatigue

From disturbed sleep due to nighttime symptoms

Severe Symptoms

Rapid breathing, difficulty speaking, blue lips

üß™ Triggers

Common asthma triggers include:

Allergens

Pollen, dust mites, pet dander, mold spores

Irritants

Smoke, air pollution, strong odors, cold air

Physical Activity

Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction

Respiratory Infections

Colds, flu, and other viral infections

üõ°Ô∏è Management

Medications

  • Quick-relief inhalers (bronchodilators)
  • Long-term control medications
  • Inhaled corticosteroids
  • Biologic therapies for severe asthma

Environmental Control

  • Use allergen-proof mattress covers
  • Maintain low indoor humidity
  • Clean regularly to reduce dust
  • Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke

Lifestyle

  • Regular exercise with proper warm-up
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Manage stress and anxiety
  • Get vaccinated against flu and pneumonia

Action Plan

  • Work with your doctor to create a plan
  • Monitor symptoms with peak flow meter
  • Know your triggers and avoid them
  • Recognize and treat attacks early

üè• Emergency Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if you experience:

  • Severe breathlessness or wheezing
  • No improvement after using rescue inhaler
  • Difficulty speaking in full sentences
  • Lips or fingernails turning blue
  • Rapid worsening of symptoms
