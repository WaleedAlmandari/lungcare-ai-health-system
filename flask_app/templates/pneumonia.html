Pneumonia

Understanding pneumonia: symptoms, causes, prevention, and treatment of this common lung infection.

Visual Comparison of Healthy vs. Pneumonia-Affected Lungs

üß¨ Introduction

Pneumonia is a lung infection that inflames the air sacs (alveoli) in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, causing symptoms such as cough with phlegm, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. It can range from mild to life-threatening and is especially serious for infants, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.

ü´Å How It Affects the Lungs

Pneumonia causes inflammation of the alveoli, the tiny air sacs where gas exchange occurs. When these sacs are filled with fluid or pus, oxygen has trouble reaching the bloodstream. This impairs breathing and can lead to serious complications like respiratory failure, sepsis, or lung abscesses.

Did you know? Pneumonia causes about 15% of all deaths in children under 5 years old worldwide.

üîµ Symptoms

Cough

Persistent cough (often with phlegm or pus)

Fever

High fever, sweating and shaking chills

Breathing

Shortness of breath, rapid breathing

Chest Pain

Chest pain when breathing or coughing

Fatigue

Extreme tiredness and weakness

Other

Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea (especially in children)

üß™ Causes

Pneumonia is caused by a variety of organisms including:

Bacteria

Most commonly Streptococcus pneumoniae (pneumococcus) in adults

Viruses

Including influenza, respiratory syncytial virus (RSV), and SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)

Fungi

More common in people with chronic health problems or weakened immune systems

Aspiration

From inhaling food, drink, vomit, or saliva into the lungs

üõ°Ô∏è Prevention

Vaccination

  • Pneumococcal vaccines (PCV13 and PPSV23)
  • Annual flu vaccine
  • COVID-19 vaccines
  • Hib vaccine for children

Hygiene

  • Wash hands regularly with soap
  • Use alcohol-based hand sanitizer
  • Cover mouth when coughing/sneezing
  • Avoid sharing personal items

Lifestyle

  • Don't smoke - it damages lung defenses
  • Get adequate sleep
  • Eat a balanced diet
  • Exercise regularly

For High-Risk Groups

  • Extra precautions during flu season
  • Prompt treatment of respiratory infections
  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Consider wearing masks in crowded places

  • Chest pain
  • Persistent fever (102¬∞F/39¬∞C or higher)
  • Persistent cough, especially with pus
  • Confusion (especially in older adults)
