COVID-19

Understanding COVID-19: Symptoms, prevention, and treatment of this respiratory illness caused by SARS-CoV-2.

How COVID-19 Affects the Respiratory System

üß¨ Introduction

COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. First identified in December 2019, it primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. While many experience mild to moderate symptoms, COVID-19 can cause severe respiratory illness, especially in older adults and those with underlying medical conditions.

Note: COVID-19 information evolves rapidly. Consult official health sources for the latest guidance.

ü´Å How It Affects the Lungs

COVID-19 primarily targets the respiratory system, causing:

  • Inflammation of lung tissue (pneumonia)
  • Damage to alveoli (air sacs) reducing oxygen exchange
  • Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) in severe cases
  • Blood clotting issues affecting lung function
  • Potential long-term lung damage ("long COVID")

Did you know? As of 2023, COVID-19 has caused over 6.9 million deaths worldwide, with hundreds of millions of confirmed cases.

üß™ Transmission & Risk Factors

How COVID-19 spreads and who's at higher risk:

Respiratory Droplets

From coughs, sneezes, or talking

Surface Contact

Touching contaminated surfaces then face

High-Risk Groups

Older adults, immunocompromised, chronic conditions

Crowded Settings

Poorly ventilated indoor spaces

üõ°Ô∏è Prevention

Vaccination

  • Stay up-to-date with recommended vaccines
  • Boosters for eligible individuals
  • Reduces severe illness risk

Hygiene

  • Frequent handwashing (20+ seconds)
  • Hand sanitizer (60%+ alcohol)
  • Avoid touching face

Protective Measures

  • Well-fitting masks in high-risk settings
  • Physical distancing when appropriate
  • Improve indoor ventilation

If Infected

  • Isolate according to current guidelines
  • Monitor symptoms closely
  • Seek medical care if symptoms worsen

üè• Treatment Options

Antiviral Medications

  • Paxlovid (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir)
  • Remdesivir (IV treatment)
  • Molnupiravir (alternative)

Hospital Care

  • Oxygen therapy
  • Immunomodulators (dexamethasone)
  • Mechanical ventilation if severe

Home Care

  • Rest and hydration
  • Fever reducers (acetaminophen)
  • Pulse oximeter monitoring

Long COVID Management

  • Multidisciplinary rehabilitation
  • Pulmonary therapy
  • Cognitive therapies if needed

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent chest pain/pressure

  • Inability to wake/stay awake
  • Pale/gray/blue skin, lips, or nail beds
  • Oxygen saturation below 90%
